1.1

Describe the purpose of IT in modern businesses

The purpose of IT in modern businesses has many uses. The existence of IT is what allows computers and peripherals such as printers and scanners to work. With IT improving over the years, it has made businesses be able to operate more efficiently. The use of computers such as desktop machines, laptops and smart phones allow the users to input and manipulate data, create digital documents, conduct research and many more. Overall, it helps to organise businesses whilst reducing the work load and increasing accuracy.

1.2

Describe the methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

The skills involved in digital project management are: strong communication skills, problem solver, and strong organisation skills.

Strong communication skills are important, especially when working in a team. By communicating with your team mates, you’ll be able to balance out the work load between one another by organising specific who completes what task. This leads to the team working more efficiently, and as a result, the work being completed faster.

Having the ability to problem solve would help you when working on your own task or project. This will prevent you from having to constantly ask for help from someone in your team. This means that the individual who is spending time helping you, would lose time completing their own task. This would overall slow down the speed in which the project is being completed.

Organisation skills would benefit individuals in being more productive in their work. By creating a plan, you’ll be able to keep on track what you have and haven’t done, as well as help break down large projects which would be helpful if working in a team. An example of a tool you could use is Trello, which is a web-based Kanban styled list making application.

There are many reasons which may lead to the failure of a digital project. One example is the lack of management. This leads to parts of the team having a lot of freedom in their work which may end up not following their business objectives, resulting in confusion and lack of interest. Lack of planning can also result in the failure of a digital project. The aims the business is planning to achieve would be foggy and unclear to the team, resulting in confusion.

1.7

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

GDPR (The General Data Protection Regulation) is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy for citizens of the European Union and the European Economic Area.

GDPR affects any company which collects personal data of EU citizens such as: name, address, cookie data, and more. Some of the key privacy and data protection requirements of the GDPR include the following:

- Requiring the consent of subjects for data processing

- The collected data must be anonymised to protect privacy

- Provide data breach notifications

- Safely handling the transfer of data across borders
