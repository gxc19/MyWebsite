1.5

Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright are legal rights which are given to the owner in order to protect their work. This prevents others from trying to use the authors work without their permission. The types of work which can be protected by copyright include: books, paintings, photographs, music, films, songs, computer software, and many more.

Copyright and other intellectual property laws affect websites because it may restrict the web-designer on what they are able to publish on the website, such as adding images off of Google. In order for the web-designer to avoid this from happening is to have permission from the author/creator of the source. This would save any legal actions which may occur in the future. Adding a reference, where you found the source would also help.
